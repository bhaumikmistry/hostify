The Monsoon

India's Seasonal Lifeline

Origin & Definition

Monsoon: Seasonal reversal of winds from High Pressure → Low Pressure that causes rainfall.

Pre-Monsoon (Mar-Apr)

ITCZ (Inter-Tropical Convergence Zone) shifts towards India. Moist local winds from BOB & Arabian Sea bring local showers.

Local names: Kaal Baisakhi (WB), Bordoichila/Nor'westers (Assam), Cherry/Coffee Showers (Karnataka), Mango Showers (Ker, TN, Karnataka)

Factors for Arrival

  • Tibetan Plateau heats up → sub-tropical jet → anticyclonic circulation
  • Sub-Tropical Jet splits; southern branch converts to tropical jet
  • Tropical Jet forms High Pressure over Indian Ocean
  • ITCZ/Monsoon Trough forms Low Pressure over India; winds flow High Pressure → Low Pressure

Burst of Monsoon

Arabian Sea branch strikes Malabar Coast of Kerala on 1st June as S-W Monsoon. Torrential rainfall with thunder & lightning.

Two Branches (S-W)

Branch Details

I. Arabian Sea Branch (3 sub-branches)

1st Sub-branch

  • Obstructed by the Western Ghats
  • 250-400 cm rainfall on Western Ghats
  • Formation of rain-shadow region on Telangana Plateau on the Eastern side of Western Ghats

2nd Sub-branch

  • Strikes the coast north of Mumbai
  • Rainfall in the Narmada & Tapi river valleys between the Vindhyas mountain range and Satpuda mountain range
  • 15 cm rainfall in Chota Nagpur Plateau
  • Meets the Bay of Bengal branch on the Gangetic Plain

3rd Sub-branch

  • Saurashtra Peninsula & Kutch
  • 250 cm of rain on Konsa coast, 200 cm on Konkan coast
  • Pune: rain-shadow, hence receives only 50-60 cm rainfall
  • Saurashtra-Kutch: Aravalli ranges being parallel to the monsoon winds, only 50-60 cm
  • 90-100 cm of rain falls after hitting the Shivalik Range

II. Bay of Bengal Branch

Clashes with Arakanyoma & forms 2 branches

1st Sub-branch

  • Meghalaya Plateau receives over 300 cm of rainfall (Mawsynram, Cherrapunji)

2nd Sub-branch

  • 250 cm in West Bengal, 200 cm in Bihar
  • 130 cm in Rewa-Panna Plateau of MP
  • Meets Arabian Sea branch at Shivaliks

Monsoon Break

No rain for 2 weeks during monsoon season.

  • Decreasing tropical cyclones
  • ITCZ at Shivalik foothills
  • Winds parallel to west coast
  • Temperature inversion in Rajasthan

Withdrawal (Oct-Nov)

Sun's Dakshinayana (southward shift). High Pressure over India, Low Pressure over Indian Ocean. Direction: NE → SW. Called North-East Monsoon. Tamil Nadu coast gets ~100cm. Dry evergreen forests.

Mawatha (Western Disturbances)

In December, sub-tropical jet blows over Ganga plains (Punjab, HR). Absorbs moisture from Mediterranean & Black Sea. Warm in nature.

These winds get struck by the Himalayas & cause rainfall in N-India along with other parts (sometimes).

  • Months: Oct-Nov
  • Direction: West → East (Westerlies)
  • J&K 20cm, Punjab 15cm, Allahabad 10cm, Patna 15cm
  • Importance: Essential for Rabi crops. Ensures availability of water throughout the year as it prevents dry spells during the dry winter months.
