What this model tells you

The honest math

At 220 transactions/day and $8.50 avg ticket, EBITDA is barely positive in Year 2. This is a tight model. You need volume OR higher tickets to make real money.

The real risk

~30% of coffee shops close in year 1. The #1 cause is undercapitalization. This model budgets $168K in working capital specifically for the ramp period cash burn.

Cover

Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn · ~1,200 sq ft second-generation retail · 24-month lease

Why This Location & Structure

Prime Williamsburg/DUMBO retail runs $100-200+/sq ft and breaks the 10-15% rent-to-revenue rule for a new cafe. Bed-Stuy corridor offers $50-68/sf with strong foot traffic and growing specialty coffee demand.

A 12-24 month term is a pop-up/short-term deal, not a standard 5-10 yr retail lease. This changes the capital structure, the landlord relationship, and the exit strategy. Prove concept → build brand → negotiate long-term or relocate with leverage.
